Quick CAN history

  • CAN CC (CAN classic): Was invented by Bosch 1986 (SAE Paper 860391) and since then widely used in automotive and industrial applications. Supports up to 8 byte payload and 1 Mbit/s.
  • CAN FD (Flexible Data-Rate): Introduced 2012 by Bosch. Supports up to 64 byte payload and bit rates up to 8 Mbit/s in the data phase (payload).
  • CAN XL (eXtended Length): Specified in ISO 11898-1:2024. Supports up to 2048 byte payload and bit rates up to 20 Mbit/s in the data phase (payload). Beside this it has many further features: as example Ethernet tunneling. So CAN XL is the better physcial layer for Ethernet (up to 20 Mbit/s)
